The Atma Bodha is Shankara at his most distilled. Where the Vivekachudamani extends to nearly six hundred verses, the Atma Bodha reduces the entire teaching to sixty-eight. It moves through the same territory, the discrimination of the Self from what is not the Self, the analysis of the five sheaths, the recognition of pure awareness, but does so with the economy of a finished pointing.

The text opens with an image that has become emblematic. As the rising sun dispels the darkness, so the dawning of self-knowledge dispels the ignorance that takes the apparent self for the real Self.

## Opening verses

### 1
This treatise on Self-knowledge is composed for those who have through asceticism purified themselves of all sin and have become tranquil and free from desires, and who long for liberation.

### 2
Just as fire is the direct cause of cooking, so without knowledge there can be no liberation. Compared with all other forms of discipline, knowledge of the Self is the one direct means of liberation.

### 3
Action cannot destroy ignorance, for action is not in conflict with ignorance. Knowledge alone destroys ignorance, as light destroys deep darkness.
