The contemplative current of early Christianity, suppressed by the fourth-century Church but preserved in the Nag Hammadi library, the Pistis Sophia, and the Hermetic dialogues. Where the canonical gospels speak of belief and obedience, the Gnostic texts speak of *gnosis* — direct, experiential knowledge — and locate the kingdom of God within the soul of the seeker.
